Apple Inc. is an American multinational technology company based in Cupertino, California, known for its consumer electronics, software, and services. Founded in 1976 by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne, it started as Apple Computer Company and became Apple Inc. in 2007, reflecting its broader focus beyond computers. Apple rose to prominence with products like the Apple II, Macintosh, iMac, iPod, iPhone, and iPad, and it also launched the iTunes Store. After struggling in the 1990s, it rebounded under Jobs, who returned to lead the company back to profitability. Tim Cook succeeded Jobs in 2011 after his death. Apples product range includes the iPhone, iPad, Apple Watch, and services like Apple Pay and Apple Music. It is a leading tech company by revenue, valued at over $3.2 trillion as of June 2024, and is recognized for its strong brand loyalty despite facing criticism over labor practices and business ethics.
